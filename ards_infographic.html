Berlin Definition (2012) Criteria

Timing

Onset within 1 week of known clinical insult or new/worsening respiratory symptoms

Chest Imaging

Bilateral opacities not fully explained by effusions, collapse, or nodules

Oedema Origin

Respiratory failure not fully explained by cardiac failure or fluid overload

Oxygenation

PaO₂/FiO₂ ≤ 300 mmHg with PEEP or CPAP ≥ 5 cmH₂O

⚠️
Common Causes
Direct (Pulmonary)
  • Pneumonia (bacterial, viral, fungal)
  • Aspiration of gastric contents
  • Pulmonary contusion / inhalation injury
  • Near drowning
Indirect (Extrapulmonary)
  • Sepsis (most common cause overall)
  • Major trauma / shock
  • Pancreatitis, blood transfusion (TRALI)
  • Drug overdose / burns

🔬
Pathophysiology Stages
Phase 1 · 0–6 hrs
Exudative Phase
Inflammatory cascade → alveolar-capillary barrier breakdown → protein-rich oedema floods alveoli → surfactant dysfunction → atelectasis
Phase 2 · Days 3–7
Proliferative Phase
Type II pneumocyte proliferation, fibroblast activation, early fibrosis, ongoing hypoxaemia
Phase 3 · Week 2–3
Fibrotic Phase
Collagen deposition, bullae formation, risk of pneumothorax; recovery or chronic respiratory impairment
🩺
Clinical Features
  • Severe progressive dyspnoea and tachypnoea
  • Refractory hypoxaemia (SpO₂ low despite O₂)
  • Bilateral crackles on auscultation
  • Cyanosis, use of accessory muscles
  • Diffuse bilateral infiltrates on CXR / CT
  • Decreased lung compliance
🔍
Diagnostic Workup
  • ABG — PaO₂/FiO₂ ratio, pH, PaCO₂
  • Chest X-Ray — bilateral opacities, "white-out"
  • CT Chest — heterogeneous consolidation (gravity-dependent)
  • Echo / BNP — exclude cardiogenic pulmonary oedema
  • Cultures / PCR — identify causative organism
  • BAL — differential cell count, protein if needed
💊
Management Pillars
Ventilation Strategy
  • Lung-protective ventilation (ARDSnet)
  • Tidal volume 6 ml/kg IBW
  • Plateau pressure < 30 cmH₂O
  • Optimal PEEP titration
  • Permissive hypercapnia acceptable
  • Prone positioning ≥ 16 h/day (severe)
  • High-flow nasal cannula (mild–moderate)
Supportive Care
  • Conservative fluid strategy
  • Daily spontaneous breathing trials
  • Light sedation (RASS −1 to 0 target)
  • Early mobilisation / physio
  • DVT prophylaxis
  • Stress ulcer prophylaxis
  • Nutritional support (enteral preferred)
Adjunctive / Rescue
  • Neuromuscular blockade (cisatracurium)
  • Inhaled nitric oxide / prostacyclin
  • High-frequency oscillatory ventilation
  • Extracorporeal membrane oxygenation (ECMO)
  • Corticosteroids (selected cases)
  • Treat underlying cause (e.g. antibiotics)
  • Lung recruitment manoeuvres
🚨
Complications
  • Ventilator-induced lung injury (VILI)
  • Pneumothorax / barotrauma
  • Ventilator-associated pneumonia (VAP)
  • Multi-organ dysfunction syndrome (MODS)
  • Pulmonary fibrosis (long-term)
  • ICU-acquired weakness / PTSD
📈
Prognosis & Recovery
  • Most survivors extubated within 2–4 weeks
  • Lung function often returns to near-normal by 6–12 months
  • Persistent fatigue, weakness, and cognitive issues common
  • Younger patients and direct lung injury have better outcomes
  • Severity of underlying illness is the main mortality driver
  • Mortality has improved significantly since lung-protective ventilation
