## Context
Creates a summary of a haematology patient's medical information from an infectious diseases perspective that I can put into the patient's records.
It begins with a narrative prose summary and is then followed by structured sections.

# Section 1: Narrative Summary
Begin with a concise narrative summary of the case written in prose (one to two paragraphs). It should read as a flowing clinical narrative, not bullet points.
The narrative should give an overview of:
- Who the patient is and their underlying haematological diagnosis
- Their transplant / treatment status and where they are in their disease course
- The main current infectious disease issue(s) prompting review
- The overall clinical trajectory and current status

Rules
- Write in prose, not bullet points
- Do not include laboratory values
- Keep it concise and readable, so a clinician can grasp the gist of the case in a few sentences
- Do not use em dashes anywhere in the output

Example of a good narrative summary:
```
Mr X is a gentleman with multiply relapsed/refractory DLBCL transformed from marginal zone lymphoma, now status post haploidentical allogeneic HSCT (D0 05/12/2025) with his son as donor in a high-risk CMV donor-negative/recipient-positive setting. His post-transplant course has been complicated by recurrent RSV, HHV-6 DNAaemia and presumed GI GvHD. He is currently admitted with CMV reactivation that has broken through Letermovir prophylaxis, without evidence of end-organ disease, and is responding to IV Ganciclovir and CMV Megalotect. His course has also been complicated by a second non-neutropenic fever and a provoked segmental pulmonary embolism for which he is anticoagulated. Overall he remains clinically stable and non-toxic, with CMV viral load now trending down.
```

# Section 3: Timeline
The next section should consist of a timeline of events. The timeline should be formatted as a table, with the first column being date and the second column being important events that happened during that date. Follow the following rules
- Do not use bullet points within other bullet points
- Each sentence in second column should be separated by ;
- Do not include citations
- Output the table at the bottom of the target page.
- Do not use the | character anywhere in the timeline; use the date followed by a colon format shown below
- Do not include laboratory values, except for CRP, procalcitonin (procal) and total white (TW/WBC), which should be filled in where available for that date
- Use the word "started" rather than "commenced"

Example of a good timeline:
```
08/04/26: Elective admission for TIL infusion; Started on Acyclovir and Bactrim prophylaxis; CRP 12; procal 0.2; TW 4.1
```

# Section 7: Impression
For each infectious disease issue, write a short clinical narrative that explains the reasoning behind the diagnosis rather than restating numbers and investigation results. Each point should convey:
- What the working diagnosis is and why it is the leading explanation
- The clinical reasoning that supports it, including the host/immune context and how the picture fits together
- What has been considered and argued against, and the degree of diagnostic certainty
- The reasoning behind the current management approach and how the patient is responding

Rules
- Focus on clinical thoughts and interpretation, not on listing laboratory values, culture results, or radiology findings (those already appear in the Objective section)
- Avoid copying and pasting investigations; refer to them only briefly when they directly support the reasoning
- Keep each issue to a few sentences of clear clinical reasoning
- Do not use em dashes
- Use the word "started" rather than "commenced"

# Section 8: Suggest
Give a brief list of sensible suggestions from infectious disease point of view

# Output Format
The entire ID Haematology Summary should be output into a plain text code box, beginning with the narrative summary in prose, followed by the structured sections.
Do not include laboratory units
Use SI units for all laboratory values, and express CD4 counts in ×10⁶/L

The plan is observation with a dermatology opinion rather than empirical treatment, given the diagnostic uncertainty and absence of systemic features.
