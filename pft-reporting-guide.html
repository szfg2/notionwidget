Grading conventions

Severity across spirometry (FEV1), DLCO and lung volumes (TLC) is graded on the z-score of the relevant index against GLI reference values. LLN = lower limit of normal (z = −1.64, 5th centile); ULN = upper limit of normal (z = +1.64).

Normalz > −1.64 (> LLN)
Mild−1.64 to −2.5
Moderate−2.51 to −4
Severe< −4

Obstruction

Defined by FEV1/FVC < LLN. Severity is then graded on the FEV1 z-score.

Restriction

Reduced TLC < LLN on lung volumes. Severity graded on the TLC z-score. Cannot be diagnosed from spirometry alone.

Bronchodilator response

ERS/ATS 2022: significant if change in FEV1 and/or FVC > 10% of predicted after 400µg salbutamol.

Bronchodilator on oscillometry

Significant if any one: R5 −32%, X5 +44%, or AX −65%.

Interpretation algorithm

Start from the FEV1/FVC ratio, then bring in lung volumes and DLCO to resolve the pattern.

  1. Is FEV1/FVC < LLN? If yes → obstructive process. Grade severity on FEV1 z-score. Check RV/TLC for air-trapping and TLC for a co-existing restrictive (mixed) defect.
  2. FEV1/FVC ≥ LLN with low FEV1 and/or FVC? Non-obstructive reduction — do not call restriction on spirometry alone. Report PRISm and recommend DLCO + lung volumes.
  3. Add lung volumes (TLC). TLC < LLN with normal RV/TLC → simple restriction. TLC < LLN with high RV/TLC → complex restriction. TLC normal despite low FEV1/FVC → non-specific ventilatory impairment (NSVI).
  4. Add DLCO. Grade on z-score; report severity from the uncorrected value. Helps separate parenchymal (low DLCO) from extra-parenchymal restriction and vascular disease.
  5. Inspect the flow-volume loop. Limb truncation localizes upper-airway obstruction (see FV loop table).
  6. Confirm bronchodilator responsiveness / provocation as indicated. BD response, methacholine/saline challenge, exercise, FeNO for the asthma phenotype.
If spirometry is normal but TLC is reduced, report as: "Normal spirometry but lung volumes show mild/moderate/severe simple/complex restriction."
